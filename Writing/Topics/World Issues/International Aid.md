Full Essay

Some think that the authorities should only offer aid to their own people instead of assisting other countries. In my opinion, I disagree with this viewpoint since providing assistance to other countries is a global responsibility and can benefit donor nations in many aspects. 

Aiding people in developing countries is a moral obligation. Undeniably, natural disasters such as earthquakes, floods, or droughts can cause unprecedented destruction, often catching governments off guard and taking a serious toll on the lives and property of local residents. Refusing to give aid to countries that lack sufficient infrastructure could result in hundreds of thousands of unnecessary deaths, creating a humanitarian crisis that cannot be ignored by other powerful countries. In addition, unsupported regions may struggle with extreme poverty, infectious diseases, or high crime rates. If left unsupported, these threats would go beyond national borders and ultimately have severe and far-reaching consequences for every corner of the globe. Donor nations can stabilize these regions, reducing the international inequality and resentment that often fuel global conflict. 

Moreover, offering assistance to others can bring plenty of benefits to donor nations. As developing countries start growing economically from international aid in the agriculture, fishing, and manufacturing industries, a new emerging market could be opened up for donor nations. This could benefit businesses in donor nations because they can get more business opportunities beforehand. Beyond these fundamental sectors, enterprises in donor nations involved in space, biotechnology (or medical), or advanced manufacturing can further refine their expertise and products through the unique challenges encountered abroad. The resulting versatility, robustness, and cost-efficiency of these improved products would make it easier to stand out to potential markets and create substantial revenue. Such strategic investment could ultimately enhance a nation’s global influence and elevate its international profile.

In conclusion, although focusing exclusively on citizens’ welfare can bring short-term domestic benefits, the authorities should not limit their responsibility to their own borders. Not only is the assistance to others one of their duties, but it also uplifts their international reputation and impact.
